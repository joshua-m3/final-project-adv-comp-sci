Introduction to the bass guitar:

The bass guitar is very similar to a normal guitar with a few key differences. Primarily, it is a 4 stringed instrument with anywhere from 20-24 frets (the metal bars separating the rectangles). These frets represent moving up or down the musical alphabet which is comprised of 8 notes with a sharp/flat in between each (except between B & C and E & F). This intrument is played by finger plucking the strigs while holding down the frets to create musical sound. A bass guitar is characterized by its low sound, as it is tuned a full octave down from a guitar (an octive is 8 musical notes). This makes it useful for rhythm and low end sound. There are many different types of bass guitars as well, which include the acoustic, a hollow body wooden bass guitar, the electric, a solid body bass guitar played through an amplifier, or an upright bass, a large hollow body wooden bass played standing up. Each guitar has immortalized itself in the industry and music history
