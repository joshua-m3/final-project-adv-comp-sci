Introduction to the Piano:

A piano is a keyboard instrument that produces sound when its keys are depressed, activating an action mechanism where hammers strike strings. Modern pianos have a row of 88 black and white keys. Pianos are reserved for the original large pianos made from maple, mahogany, rosewoods, Brazil woods or ebony woods. There are also electric pianos that use less keys and are amplified. These make pianos much more cheap and space efficent.
